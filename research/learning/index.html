Game Design Research

I decided to read Game Design UX Best Practices by Amir Dori. Since UI/UX design is something that I want to pursue and focus on, I thought in terms of the game this is what I should focus on. There was a lot of interesting in this article regarging usability. There's little things that are so obvious but wouldnt have thought of myself such as placing UI elements in easy ti reach thumb areas since that's where the most interaction happens especially with pressing buttons. And most people are right handed so putting something in that area that will be pressed on a lot would probably be most effective. Designers also use sliding, pop ups and animations to in order to guide users to important content and actions. Effective interfaces also use color psychology, button placement as visual cues to subtly encourage desired player decisions.

The article emphasizes that interactive systems should feel intuitive and rewarding. Sliders could reveal hidden content through partial visibility and movement. While draggable items, should avoid being hidden by the player’s finger. Pop ups work best when they clearly connect to the action that triggered them and avoid cluttered dismissal options like multiple close buttons. Rewarded video ads are most successful when they provide meaningful prizes that players perceive as valuable.

Strategies that could improve long term satisfaction and monetization for the player. This could be short intro videos that are skippable and can be replayed at a later time instead of appearing repeatedly. “Rate Us” prompts should only appear after players experience moments of success to increase positive reviews. Although personally I always found this annoying in apps but it would be a good way to receive feedback and know how it could improve. As with all UI/UX strong calls to action are essential and that goes for gaming as well. Overall, the article presents mobile game UX as a combination of psychology, accessibility, and data driven design choices.
